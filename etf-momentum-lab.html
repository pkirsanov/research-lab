Return under market regimes CAPM-implied: E[fund] = annual α + β × market return

Regime Market 1Y % Probability
Set each regime's 1-year market (benchmark) return and its probability. Each fund's expected return is its CAPM line off the chosen benchmark; the blended EV is probability-weighted. Bands use residual (idiosyncratic) volatility. Probabilities are normalised to sum to 1 for the expected value (the all-zero case is guarded). CAPM assumes a stable linear, stationary fund-vs-benchmark relationship (constant α, β) — betas estimated from the window may not persist.

Model assumption: constant drift μ and volatility σ estimated from the window. The default Gaussian (GBM) shocks are thin-tailed; switch Shocks to Student-t or historical bootstrap to capture the fat tails and skew real markets show. Even then, volatility is held constant and paths are educational — not a forecast.
