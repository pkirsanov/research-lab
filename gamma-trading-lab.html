Methodology (short). Gamma exposure (GEX) per strike = Black-Scholes gamma × open interest × 100 × spot² × 1%, calls positive / puts negative under the default dealer convention (dealers long calls, short puts — toggle in Power). The gamma-flip (JEX) is the price where net GEX crosses zero: above it dealers are long-gamma and suppress volatility (pin); below it they are short-gamma and amplify it (trend/waterfall). Days-in-regime and the OVI percentile accrue from a rolling daily snapshot kept in your browser (no paid history API). OVI weights each contract's volume by its gamma, nets calls vs puts into a ratio (direction) and a weighted quantity (impact), and ranks the impact against prior snapshots. The expiration cycle maps the monthly and quarterly OPEX (third Friday), the 14- and 30-day windows, and a max-pain-anchored pin-vs-overextension read. Snapshots are reused from — and mirrored back to — the Options Structure Lab's local store, so nothing is re-fetched.
