Session and FX map

The local cash close usually precedes the US ETF close

A same-date US ETF bar is not a synchronized local-market observation. Asia may be nearly a full session old by the New York close; Europe is several hours old; Americas overlap more. FX trades longer and can move after the home exchange shuts.

Method in one screen

No stored signals · all calculations run in this page
Relative momentum

Trailing 21-, 63-, and 126-day ETF returns minus the selected control. The chosen horizon gets 55% of the momentum sub-score; the other two split the balance.

Trend and risk

Price versus 20/50/200-day averages, moving-average ordering, 63-day realized volatility, and 252-bar max drawdown. Risk posture changes weights, not observations.

FX is confirmation

The US-listed ETF return already embeds currency translation. Local-currency strength is scored separately as confirmation; raising its weight can double-count the same currency impulse.
