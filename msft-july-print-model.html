Cost cycle & market structure — the multi-year overlay

Thesis verdict: largely TRUE, but the timeline is longer than "some horizons" implies. Hardware-cost inflation favors scale players and is structural through ~2027–2028 (some see 2030), so the consolidation tailwind and the margin headwind both persist for years. For MSFT it is double-edged: near-term margin pressure from input inflation + depreciation, offset by share gains from consolidation and its own Maia silicon as the eventual deflation lever.

How the math works

Each growth lever contributes cash gross profit (ex-depreciation); the depreciation step and incremental opex are subtracted to isolate the capital cost of growth:

GP_price = RevFY26 × price% × priceMargin (no capex → clean offset)
GP_volume = RevFY26 × volume% × volumeMargin (this is what creates the depreciation)
GP_fx = RevFY26 × fx% × 95% · GP_churn = −RevFY26 × churn% × churnMargin
ΔOpex = opexIntensity × (price$ + volume$) · ΔDep = your input
OI₂₇ = OI₂₆ + GP_price + GP_volume + GP_fx − GP_churn − ΔDep − ΔOpex
EPS = (OI₂₇ + otherIncome) × (1 − tax) ÷ shares  (operating-style; excludes OpenAI mark-to-market)
