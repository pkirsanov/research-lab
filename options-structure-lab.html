Dealer positioning & option walls

📊 Net GEX by strike
Signed dealer gamma per strike (green = positive/pinning, red = negative/amplifying). The gamma-flip line is where cumulative dealer gamma crosses zero; the dashed line is spot.

Net GEX as spot varies — the zero-crossing is the gamma flip; the slope is pinning strength. Right axis: shares dealers must trade per 1% move to stay hedged (they buy dips / sell rips in long-gamma, and chase in short-gamma).

Where contracts are stacked. The largest call-OI strike above spot is the Call Wall (resistance); the largest put-OI strike below spot is the Put Wall (support).
🔥 Volume by strike + Vol/OI
Today's traded volume by strike (calls/puts). Amber outline = Vol/OI > 1 (unusual — new positioning, not just resting OI). The tallest total-volume strike is the HVL (high-volume level).
↕ OI change vs last snapshot
Build (bar up) vs unwind (bar down) since your previous fetch on a different day — accrues from local history, no paid API. Calls building above spot = fuel to break resistance; puts building below = a firmer floor.
📉 Volume profile (cash) + POC
Where shares actually traded over the window (not options): the POC (point of control) is the highest-volume price = a magnet; the shaded band is the 70% value area. Fuses cash S/R with the option walls above.

Greeks, volatility & expirations

Δ Γ greeks by strike
Per-strike net gamma (bars) and net delta exposure (line) — computed in-browser via Black-Scholes. Gamma peaks near the walls; delta exposure shows the directional tilt of positioning.
〽️ Vanna & charm by strike
Second-order exposures: vanna (∂Δ/∂vol — how hedging shifts when IV moves) and charm (∂Δ/∂time — hedging drift into expiry, the OPEX pin pressure).
🙂 IV smile + term structure
Front-expiry implied-vol smile across strikes (curve) and ATM IV by expiry (markers) — contango (rising) is normal; backwardation (falling, near-term fear) flags stress. Put-side lift = downside skew.
🎯 Expected-move cones by expiry
±1σ expected move (from the ATM straddle / ATM IV) for each fetched expiry — how far the options market thinks price can travel by each date. Nearer expiry = tighter cone.
🧲 Max-pain curve (front expiry)
Total dollar value of in-the-money options at each candidate settle price; the minimum is Max Pain — where option holders lose most and price is often magnetically drawn near expiry. A heuristic, not a forecast.
🗓️ Expiration ladder (|GEX| weight)
Absolute gamma weight carried by each expiry — where the hedging pressure concentrates. Big front-expiry weight (0DTE/weeklies) means levels there dominate the tape.

How to read it

The one question. Where is resistance/support, and will price PIN there or BREAK through? The walls and max pain tell you the levels; the gamma-flip regime tells you whether they hold.

Call Wall / Put Wall. The strikes with the most call OI above spot / put OI below spot. Dealers hedging that OI tend to defend these — the Call Wall acts as resistance, the Put Wall as support.

Gamma flip & regime. Above the flip (net positive dealer gamma) dealers sell rallies / buy dips → volatility is suppressed, price pins. Below the flip (negative gamma) they chase → volatility amplifies, walls break and momentum runs.

GEX sign is an assumption. The default assumes dealers are long calls / short puts. It is not observable — the toggle exists because a wrong sign flips the regime read. Sanity-check against the tape.

Max pain is the settle price minimizing total option-holder value — a soft pin magnet into expiry, unreliable in short-gamma or around catalysts.

Expected-move cone = ±1σ from the ATM straddle. Price inside the cone is "normal"; a wall just outside a tight cone is a realistic target.

Volume profile POC is where the most shares traded — cash-market S/R that complements the option walls. When a wall and the POC line up, the level is stronger.

Vol/OI & OI-change separate new positioning from resting OI. Calls building above spot = fuel to break up; puts building below = a firmer floor.

Squeeze = high short%float + high days-to-cover + rising call skew + negative GEX + up price momentum. Short interest is bi-monthly and lagging — context, not a trigger.

Honesty. This is OI + volume + IV structure on delayed / EOD data — not real order flow, dark pool, or trade-level bid/ask side. Greeks are Black-Scholes estimates. Walls and max pain are magnets that fail regularly. Do your own work.

Educational only — not investment advice. Every level, greek and regime here is a hypothetical, convention-dependent estimate computed in your browser from delayed/EOD public data you fetch. Options positioning is inferred from open interest, volume and implied volatility — it is not observed dealer inventory or real-time order flow. Verify everything yourself; options can lose money fast.
