How to read this rotation vocabulary

The Relative Rotation Graph plots each entry by its strength versus the benchmark (RS-Ratio, x) and the momentum of that strength (RS-Momentum, y). Both centre at 100. Healthy rotation runs clockwise: Improving→Leading→Weakening→Lagging→ back to Improving.
Because RS-Momentum leads the RS-Ratio, the earliest actionable signals are the in-between states this tool labels for you: Basing ↑ (still lagging, but momentum turning up — early accumulation) and Peaking ⚠ (still leading, but momentum rolling over — early distribution). The Momentum acceleration panel ranks exactly this turn, and Volume / money-flow + Breadth tell you whether the underlying participation confirms the price move or is quietly diverging (a risk flag). The rotation trigger you're hunting is one area flipping Peaking (trim / exit) while another flips Basing / Improving (add / enter) — and the Correlation & divergence panel confirms it: when two things that used to move together start decoupling, capital is rotating between them.

Rotation suggestions trade the ETF proxy · individual stocks only on an extreme case

The rotation call names the tradeable vehicle — an index / sector ETF (or a group’s representative ETF), not a basket of stocks. An individual stock is flagged only when a group’s strength is extremely concentrated in one leader (large lead over its peers, at new highs, thin breadth).
