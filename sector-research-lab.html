How to read this rotation vocabulary

The Relative Rotation Graph plots each entry by its strength versus the benchmark (RS-Ratio, x) and the momentum of that strength (RS-Momentum, y). Both centre at 100. Healthy rotation runs clockwise: Improving→Leading→Weakening→Lagging→ back to Improving.
Because RS-Momentum leads the RS-Ratio, the earliest actionable signals are the in-between states this tool labels for you: Basing ↑ (still lagging, but momentum turning up — early accumulation) and Peaking ⚠ (still leading, but momentum rolling over — early distribution). The Momentum acceleration panel ranks exactly this turn, and Volume / money-flow + Breadth tell you whether the underlying participation confirms the price move or is quietly diverging (a risk flag). The rotation trigger you're hunting is one area flipping Peaking (trim / exit) while another flips Basing / Improving (add / enter) — and the Correlation & divergence panel confirms it: when two things that used to move together start decoupling, capital is rotating between them.

Rotation suggestions trade the ETF proxy · individual stocks only on an extreme case

The rotation call names the tradeable vehicle — an index / sector ETF (or a group’s representative ETF), not a basket of stocks. An individual stock is flagged only when a group’s strength is extremely concentrated in one leader (large lead over its peers, at new highs, thin breadth). Companies by sector — momentum heatmap which names are driving each sector · fetched on demand

Once the rotation read points you at a sector, this shows which member companies are actually carrying it (or quietly lagging) — the intra-sector leadership behind the ETF. Cells are coloured by the chosen metric — 1M/3M/6M/12M excess vs the benchmark, a 1/3/6/12M blend, a risk-adjusted (blend÷vol) read, or absolute return (green = leading, red = lagging) — and sized-labelled by index weight. A ▲ marks a name at a 63-day high; an RSI badge flags overbought (≥70) or oversold (≤30). Sector ETF selector — pick the vehicle which ETF to actually buy when you rotate

When you decide to rotate into a sector, this ranks its most prominent ETFs on the ten parameters that matter: size (AUM), liquidity (live dollar ADV → real tradability & tight spreads), cost (expense ratio), coverage (holdings + broad-sector vs a narrower segment slice), concentration (top-10 weight — how diversified), sector-tracking (correlation to the sector SPDR), tracking-error (how far it drifts from the sector), downside (max drawdown), yield (distribution — matters for income sectors) and risk-adjusted momentum (a Sharpe-like read over a lookback you choose — return you keep per unit of risk). The Fit score blends them into one rank — reweightable via ⚙ Fit weights — so the trade-off is visible at a glance: a big cheap broad tracker vs a smaller, higher-momentum segment fund. β and the information ratio are shown too (tilt / active-return reads, not scored). Reference numbers (size / cost / coverage / concentration / yield) rank immediately; the live metrics fill in after you fetch. All reference numbers are approximate — verify with the issuer.
