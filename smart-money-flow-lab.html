The research behind every number

Why disclosure lag is the whole game

Copy-trading disclosed filings is not front-running — you see the trade only after it is publicly filed, and the clock between the transaction and the filing is where most of the marketed “edge” quietly evaporates.

Filing Who Typical lag (transaction → public)
Form 4 Corporate insiders (officers, directors, 10% holders) ~2 business days (SEC rule)
Periodic Transaction Report (STOCK Act) Members of Congress & senior staff up to ~30–45 days after the trade
Form 13F Institutional managers > $100M up to ~45 days after quarter-end (so a January buy can surface in mid-May)

The model treats an information edge as decaying with a tunable half-life: retained fraction = 2^(−age / half-life). At age = 0 you keep 100%; at age = half-life you keep 50%; a 45-day-old signal with a 15-day half-life keeps 2^(−3) ≈ 12%. That is the honest counterweight to a “77% in three months” thumbnail.

How consensus & conviction are scored

Per ticker the tool aggregates the (filtered) trades into:

  • Breadth = log2(1 + distinct filers) — a cluster of independent buyers is worth far more than one large ticket.
  • Size = log10(1 + |net $| / 100k) — dollars matter but with sharply diminishing weight (a $10M cluster is not 100× a $100k one).
  • Freshness = the edge-decay factor at the most-recent disclosure's age.

Naive conviction = breadth × (1 + size) × freshness measured as if you acted at transaction time (lag treated as 0). Realistic conviction multiplies that by the decay factor at the average disclosure lag. Their ratio is edge retained — identical for a 2-day Form 4 cluster (keeps almost everything) and brutal for a 45-day 13F echo.

None of these are win-rates. They are a transparent, editable conviction read whose only job is to make the lag cost visible.

What the hype videos leave out (read before copying anyone)
  • Survivorship & cherry-picking. “This politician turned $X into $Y” quotes the winners after the fact. The losing legislators and the losing months rarely make the thumbnail.
  • Lag, again. 13F is a quarterly snapshot up to 45 days stale — the manager may have already exited by the time you buy.
  • Position sizing & horizon are hidden. A disclosed buy says nothing about conviction weight, hedges, options overlays, or the intended holding period.
  • Auto-executing scraped “alerts” is a security hole. Wiring a bot to trade on whatever text appears in a Discord/newsletter is textbook signal-spoofing — anyone who can post can move your money. This tool deliberately has no execution and no live scraping.
  • Disclosure ≠ endorsement. Filings are legal artifacts, not advice, and may reflect blind trusts, tax moves, or staff-managed accounts.
